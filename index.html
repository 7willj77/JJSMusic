Release Strategy

Release smarter, not just louder.

One of the biggest mistakes artists make is treating release day as the starting line — in reality, it’s the moment your preparation gets tested.

To give your track the best chance, you should submit it through Spotify for Artists at least a week before it drops. This isn’t just admin — it opens the door to Release Radar and puts your music in front of Spotify’s editorial team.

But timing alone won’t carry you. Before you submit, everything needs to be sharp — your branding, your metadata, your story around the track. When you pitch properly, you’re not just uploading a song, you’re giving context that helps Spotify understand where it fits.

Spotify isn’t focused only on how many followers you have — it’s watching how people react when your track goes live. The first 72 hours are critical. Strong save rates, repeat listens, and real traffic coming from your socials or direct shares all signal that your track is connecting.

A smaller number of highly engaged listeners will outperform a larger, passive audience every time. If your release creates genuine interest — people saving it, replaying it, sharing it — that’s when the algorithm starts to work in your favour.

The takeaway is simple: don’t just release music — build a moment around it.
